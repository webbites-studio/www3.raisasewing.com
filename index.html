My Story

I was born in the small town of Plesetsk in Northwestern Russia, where I lived for 37 years. After graduating from law school, I built a career in the legal profession. Yet throughout my life, creativity remained an essential part of who I am. From an early age, I was drawn to making things with my hands—knitting, sewing, embroidery, and various crafts. These creative pursuits brought me joy, inspiration, and a sense of fulfillment that has stayed with me throughout the years.

Creativity runs deep in my family. My great-grandfather, who lived in the countryside, knitted stockings and other necessities for his family and passed this skill on to his daughters. My grandmother, whose name was also Raisa, was an exceptionally talented craftswoman. She crocheted beautiful bedspreads, tablecloths, and decorative pieces, and she wove colorful rugs and runners from strips of fabric. At a time when handmade rugs were highly valued and not easily available, her work was greatly admired and sought after.

After losing her husband in 1945 to wounds sustained during World War II, she raised two children on her own, built her own home in the 1960s, and worked tirelessly throughout her life. I still remember helping her weave rugs on a large wooden loom. It was my grandmother who introduced me to the foundations of craftsmanship and taught me the value of patience, dedication, and quality.

My family's tradition of sewing and craftsmanship continued through the next generation. My aunt Nina was a professional seamstress, and my mother, who received formal training in sewing, lovingly made many of my holiday dresses by hand. Inspired by these remarkable women, I devoted countless hours to sewing, creating, and studying garment construction and accessory design.

Over the years, I pursued professional training to deepen my skills. I attended sewing school in Arkhangelsk, Russia, and studied with experienced instructors who shared decades of industry knowledge. Later, I earned a degree in Image Consulting from Moscow State University of Culture and Arts.

In 2009, I moved to Portland, Maine, with my American husband and established my own business. Before leaving Russia, I shipped most of the sewing equipment that I still use in my studio today. Leaving my machines behind was never an option—they have always been an important part of my creative journey.

Today, I truly love what I do. Every garment, alteration, and handcrafted accessory is an opportunity to bring joy, confidence, and beauty into someone's life. I approach each project with care, attention to detail, and a commitment to excellence, creating pieces that are both functional and meaningful. For me, sewing is more than a profession—it is a lifelong passion, a family tradition, and a way to connect with people through craftsmanship and creativity.

Service 4

Custom Accessories

Bespoke Accessories: Artistry in Detail & Memories in Every Stitch

At Raisa's Sewing & Accessories, I believe that a flawless look is defined not only by the perfect fit of a dress but by the accessories that carry meaning and a story. I offer unique solutions that transform the tailoring process into the creation of a complete, couture ensemble.

The Magic of Transformation: Custom Fabric Brooches

When I shorten the hem of your gown, precious remnants of fabric are often left behind. Instead of letting them go to waste, I transform these delicate pieces into an exquisite, handcrafted accessory.

  • Flawless Harmony: A floral brooch created from your own dress fabric matches the texture and shade perfectly, creating a sophisticated and cohesive look.
  • Personalized Accents: You can choose between a timeless gold or a sleek silver center for your flower. I then meticulously finish each piece with shimmering beads and rhinestones to add a touch of luxury.
  • For Bridesmaids: This is a particularly meaningful choice for bridal parties. Matching or stylistically coordinated brooches serve as a beautiful unifying symbol for the bridesmaids.
  • A Keepsake to Cherish: Unlike fresh flowers, this brooch will never wither. Long after the wedding day, it remains a versatile accessory for your other outfits—a jacket, a hat, or an evening coat—forever preserving the warm memories of a wonderful celebration.

The Finishing Touch: Handcrafted Clutches & Handbags

No special occasion outfit is complete without the perfect bag. I design and create exclusive clutches and handbags that serve as a natural extension of your evening or bridal look.

  • Custom Craftsmanship: I select materials that echo the elements of your gown—whether it's delicate lace, shimmering silk, or hand-embroidered pearls.
  • Functional Elegance: Each piece is created with your specific needs in mind, ensuring your accessory is as practical as it is beautiful for your most important day.

Your style is a reflection of your individuality. Let me create the accessories that tell your story.


The Thread of Time: A Dialogue Between Generations

There is a silent dialogue between the fabric of the past and the dreams of the future. When I work with a vintage 1980s wedding gown, I don't just see lace and silk; I see a story that is waiting to be retold for a new era.

The Transformation:

I reimagined the classic silhouette to fit the spirit of a modern bride, but the soul of the original gown found its new life in the details. I transformed the vintage train into a bespoke bridal purse, accented with a modern ring handle and delicate pearl embroidery.

The Philosophy:

This is more than just upcycling; it is about continuity. As the bride walks toward her future, she carries a tangible piece of history in her hands. It is a bridge between two lives and two eras, proving that beauty is never lost—it only changes form.

At Raisa's Sewing & Accessories, I believe that every stitch is a connection. I don't just create accessories; I weave together the threads of memory, ensuring that the elegance of the past remains a living part of your most beautiful moments.
